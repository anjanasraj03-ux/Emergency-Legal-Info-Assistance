üìñ Universal Human Rights ‚Äî Legal Reference
Article 9 of the International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights provides that no one shall be subjected to arbitrary arrest or detention. Everyone arrested must be promptly informed of the reasons for their arrest and of any charges against them. Anyone detained must be brought promptly before a judge and tried within a reasonable time or released. Everyone has the right to take proceedings before a court to decide on the lawfulness of their detention (habeas corpus).

ICCPR Art. 9UDHR Art. 9Habeas Corpus
All persons are equal before courts and tribunals. Everyone has the right to a fair and public hearing by a competent, independent, and impartial tribunal. Everyone charged with a criminal offence has the right to be presumed innocent, to be informed promptly of charges, to have adequate time to prepare a defence, and to have legal assistance assigned free of charge if unable to pay.

ICCPR Art. 14Fair TrialPresumption of InnocenceLegal Aid
No one shall be subjected to arbitrary or unlawful interference with their privacy, family, home, or correspondence. Law enforcement authorities require lawful authority ‚Äî typically a judicial warrant ‚Äî to enter, search, or seize property. You may refuse entry and request to see the warrant. Do not physically resist; document everything and consult a legal authority or ILAN Member Office.

ICCPR Art. 17UDHR Art. 12Search Warrant
Article 11 of the International Covenant on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights recognises the right of everyone to an adequate standard of living, including adequate housing. Forced evictions without due process, alternative housing provision, or genuine consultation are a violation of this right under international law and OHCHR General Comment No. 7.

ICESCR Art. 11OHCHR GC No. 7Forced Eviction
Article 7 recognises the right of everyone to just and favourable conditions of work, including fair wages, equal remuneration, safe and healthy working conditions, and equal opportunity for promotion. States are obligated to progressively realise these rights through legislation and enforcement. Complemented by ILO Core Conventions on freedom of association, collective bargaining, and elimination of forced and child labour.
